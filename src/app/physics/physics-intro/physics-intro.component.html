Everyday Electricity
If you're reading this on a mobile device, electricity is quite literally coursing through your hands right now.

Electricity is so integral in powering our modern world, yet it is usually taken for granted. Only in its absence do we truly appreciate its necessity and the creature comforts it provides.

Given the pervasiveness of electricity, being knowledgeable with how electricity is quantified and applied in our everyday lives is deeply beneficial.
In this lesson, you will learn:
  • The definitions and applications of energy and power quantifiers such as Watt (W) and Kilowatt-Hour (kWh)
  • How electrical energy is consumed (and sometimes stored) by your devices and appliances
  • How electrical energy is delivered from the grid to your home
  • How to read and understand your power utility bills
Fret not, for this lesson on "Everyday Electricity" assumes no prior knowledge, other than basic familiarity with electricity such as charging and using mobile devices, or using electrical appliances in the home.

In the first chapter "Watt is Love", we will be following Dionne's electrifying journey as she prepares for school in the morning.
A Primer in Kinematics
Kinematics is a branch of physics that studies the motion of bodies.

An apple falling from a tree, a satellite orbiting the Earth, an Olympic sprinter running the 100m dash - these are all bodies in motion whose geometries can be described and predicted with kinematics. Thus, kinematics is often referred to as the "geometry of motion".

In this lesson, selected fundamental topics in kinematics will be taught.
