Watt is Love
Like most college students, Dionne charges her mobile phone overnight after a long day of use.

While doing so, she notices that the charging brick is rated for 25W, and the battery capacity is 3000mAh.

Dionne recalls learning in high school that watt(W) is a unit for power, where power is the rate of change of energy measured in joules(J).

In all, 1 watt is equivalent to 1 joule per second.
But how does 25 joules per second even relate to the battery capacity of 3000mAh?

It would certainly make more sense if the battery capacity was measured in joules instead, Dionne thought. If so, the total charging time would simply be capacity divided by wattage.

As we will uncover in the later pages, there turns out to be a rather convoluted way to indirectly convert mAh to J.
Before attempting to convert mAh to J, Dionne wanted to first estimate the battery capacity in joules.

Starting with a battery percentage of 25%, Dionne records that it takes approximately 90 minutes to fully charge.

Can you estimate the battery capacity in joules?
